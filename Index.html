Overview

Project Overview

Insurance companies process millions of claims annually, making it difficult to monitor operational leakage, overpayments, and claim inefficiencies at scale.

This project analyzes ~1.25M insurance claim records to identify financial leakage, high-utilization claim behavior, operational risks, and profitability opportunities using interactive Power BI dashboards and advanced DAX modeling.

Dataset

1.25M+ insurance claim records including approvals, overpayments, diagnoses, hospitals, and patient-level attributes

Tools Used

Power BI • DAX • MySQL • Star Schema • Bookmarks • What-If Parameters • Drill-through

Best Result

₹54.68B profit opportunity identified through leakage reduction analysis

Business Value

Operational leakage monitoring • claim governance • risk-based decision support

Insights

Key Insights

Operational and financial insights identified from large-scale insurance claims analysis.

Overpayments and operational leakage significantly reduced realized profitability despite strong premium collection.

Heart Disease, Cancer, and Accident-related claims contributed the highest financial leakage exposure.

Private hospitals and standard policies generated higher overpayment contributions compared to other segments.

Smoker and overweight patient groups showed higher claim utilization and overpayment patterns.

Scenario analysis revealed a strong unrealized profit opportunity through tighter operational controls.

Deep dive decomposition trees enabled micro-level patient and hospital investigation for leakage analysis.

Recommendations

Business Recommendations

Operational and financial control recommendations based on claims leakage and overpayment analysis.

Claims Control Recommendations

Recommendations to reduce leakage, improve claim governance, and optimize profitability.

1

Double-check all claims before approval to avoid unnecessary overpayments.

2

Monitor high-frequency claims and flag unusual activity early.

3

Introduce stricter review for high-value and repeated claims.

4

Conduct regular audits and risk-based claim reviews.

5

Flag patients or hospitals with frequent claim activity for additional verification.

6

Implement automated claim limit controls to prevent payouts beyond policy coverage.

7

Deploy the dashboard for weekly claims monitoring, leakage control, and operational decision-making.

Expected outcome: Reduced operational leakage, improved claim governance, lower overpayments, and stronger profitability control.
